Enhanced Vocabulary Trees for Real-Time Object Recognition in Image and Video Streams

This project examines k-Means clustering trees in computer vision, building on Nister and Stewenius' work (CVPR 2006). It evaluates their effectiveness in visual recognition by analyzing their hierarchical structure, impact on accuracy, and computational efficiency. The implementation includes dataset setup, ORB feature detection, k-means clustering, max voting, cluster reassignment, and score calculations. Performance is tested on public datasets, with future plans for larger datasets like COCO and alternative feature detectors to improve accuracy. The goal is to enhance understanding of k-means clustering trees in the Bag of Words framework, assess their strengths and limitations, and inspire further research.
